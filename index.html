About me

I am an undergraduate at USC's Viterbi School of Engineering double majoring in Computer Science (BS) and Applied Math (BA). I love Algorithm Design, Complexity Theory, and Russian Literature. I expect to graduate in the Fall of 2022.

Research

Since the Spring of 2021, I have been working with Professor David Kempe on proving distortion bounds in the field of Metric Voting.

During the Summer and Fall of 2020, I had the great opportunity to work as an undergraduate researcher at Hughes Professor Assad Oberai's Computation and Data Driven Discovery Group (CD3). I co-authored a paper, entitled “Machine Learning Based Predictors for COVID-19 Disease Severity”, based on this work, which was published in the Journal of Scientific Reports. The paper can be found here.

My research interests include

  • Computational Complexity Theory

  • Analysis of Social Choice Mechanisms
